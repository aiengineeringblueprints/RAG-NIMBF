\section{Related Work}

RAG combines neural retrieval and sequence generation to ground model responses in external evidence \cite{lewis2020retrieval}. Evaluation commonly includes retrieval-focused metrics, answer similarity, and faithfulness-oriented measures. Frameworks such as RAGAS provide automated metrics for answer quality and context usage \cite{es2023ragas}.

Existing evaluation approaches often emphasize either retrieval quality or generation quality. Practical RAG development requires both views, because a strong generator cannot compensate for missing evidence and a strong retriever can still be undermined by poor prompting or synthesis. This work positions the benchmark framework as an experiment harness for comparing these interacting components.
